How should we approach creative collaboration with AI?

My Position

I see AI as a useful creative tool, but humans need to stay in charge of the meaning, emotion, and purpose. Working with AI this semester showed me it's great at helping me make things fasterâ€”I can test ideas quickly, try different approaches, and get past technical roadblocks. But AI doesn't actually understand what I'm making or why it matters. It can copy patterns and sound smart, but it doesn't have real experiences or cultural understanding.

So I think of it as an unequal partnership where we each bring different things:

AI is good at: speed, generating variations, spotting patterns, and iterating without getting tired. It can help me code, suggest ideas I didn't think of, and handle boring repetitive stuff.

I'm good at: knowing what problem I'm actually trying to solve, making ethical choices, understanding my audience as real people, and deciding what feels right or meaningful. I bring the lived experience and cultural context that AI can't learn from data.

The best collaboration happens when I give AI clear, specific instructions and use it to expand what I can makeâ€”but I stay critical of what it produces and make the final calls. Vague prompts get vague results. Clear goals get useful help.

Bottom line: AI can help me make more and try more, but I need to stay the author who decides what matters and why.
